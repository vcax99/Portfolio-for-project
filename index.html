Exposition

Analysis of the crisis

The victory of the Taliban has caused conflicts in the security measures of Afghanistan that invaded the economy of the country. The crisis affected politics, increased food security, and further widespread deprivation that reduced consumption and shrunk household incomes that caused sudden cessation of aid which led to a drop in aggregate demand. Afghanistan's financial status and its central bank also known as Afghanistan Bank lost its ability to manage the systems of payments and conducted monetary policies that freeze the assets further resulting in a shortage of printing new currency notes in Afghanistan (Omar and Tyagi, 2021). The resumption of the economy remained on budget and the international community during the year of war helped Afghanistan tackle the challenges that were imposed by the Taliban. The country was going through a huge debt and was bound to use a few models that helped to grow its assets and become financially stable.

Financial Liberalisation and over borrowing

The model was introduced by McKinnon along with his former student Huw Pill, to stabilize the factor of inflation that was affected by the war and perform multiple functional activities to improve the economic indicator such as exchange rate, domestic collection of revenue, and increment in exports. The argument mentions the opening of the financial sector that impacts the domestic banking sector and removes the problem of hazardous situations. According to Kuzmanet al. (2022), the statistics of Afghanistan and its preliminary official status showed a percentage of 20.7 in 2021 due to the effects of war. The functions of banks are reliant on government taxes and high inflation rates and reserve ratios produce a gap that impacts lending and deposit procedures including interests. The reduction led to 20-30% in August 2021 which resulted in the loss of jobs and impacted the common living and poverty, the bills were found to suffer from the liquidity crisis and the shortage of the bank notes were raising the problem. The economic structure of Afghanistan was 75% dependent on foreign countries, which increases the risk of over borrowing and bad debts which cannot be paid in the meantime (Benigno et al. 2022). When the Taliban took over the country the World Bank was instructed to cut around 2 billion in international assistance for the payment of salaries to the workers and cover the expenses through projected funds imposed by the international development association also known as IDA.

First and second-generation models of currency crisis

The construction of the first generation for the currency crisis was initiated with an idea that is simple to assume the factors which are wrong with the economic fundamentals such as policy inconsistency that are adopted during the fixed exchange rate, for example, an increase in monetary growth. The second generation of the model for the currency crisis is a policy that is not effective for protection from the attacks of high inflation or reduced GDP and therefore, is based on the reaction of the functional activities of the bank. The Afghanistan government's major aim was to pursue two goals that included a fixed exchange rate and reducing the element of unemployment along with the low-interest rates, payment of bad debts, and protection of the economy of the country. Opinion of Islam, (2022) stated that the war worsens the situation which resulted in 500,000 job losses and the unemployment rate increased by 11.22% in 2019 which affected the market policies. The bigger attack came when the currency was not available and due to such a crisis, the maintenance of the exchange rate was high and costly which caused major instability in the economy and achieving the goals of the domestic nation. The motive of the first-generation model is to identify the problem of the crisis whereas the second generation provides validity and points that can be used for the inheritance of the instabilities. The implication of both the first and second-generation models relates to the different types of policies that impact the private sector as well as help the economy to stabilize.

SMART Recommendations

Specific

For the first time in twenty years, the Taliban are in charge of Afghanistan. They no longer face armed opposition, but they now have an economy that is on the verge of collapse, which has the potential to exacerbate an already dire humanitarian disaster. The economy already lacked a strong foundation and was largely dependent on handouts. When foreign help accounts for 10% or more of a country's GDP, that country is said to be aid-dependent; the World Bank estimates that international aid accounted for roughly 40% of Afghanistan's GDP (Bbc, 2022). Western nations, including the United States and Germany, halted foreign aid to Afghanistan when it became evident that the Taliban would take over Kabul. In this situation, in my opinion, they have to stay strong and have to bond with another neighbouring country, from where they get help to face the problem. Since then, payments have also been suspended by the IMF and the World Bank. The “central bank of Afghanistan, Afghanistan Bank (DAB)”, has also frozen its foreign deposits. At this time, the specific goal of this country will be unity which is to give more power to fight against the situation.

The specific goal can be achieved with the help of unity and a strong mentality. Humanitarian aid cannot fix Afghanistan's humanitarian crisis on its own. To lift Afghans out of poverty, Afghanistan's economy must be upgraded. But to improve Afghanistan's economy, the International community must communicate with the Taliban.
